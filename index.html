Camp Guidelines

No W*rk talk

We want you to have the freedom to be yourself, not your job or your career. This is your chance to get to know people for who they really are and build relationships with people you can truly call your friend .

No Technology

No screens here. “Check in” your phone to “check out” at camp. Step foot into an environment that is device and technology free-a chance to live in the moment.

No Stress

Simply having fun requires zero stress. Our goal is to sponsor a stress-free environment to help you get the most benefit out of a tech-free weekend. The only status update you need is that you will be “off the grid.”

FAQ

What are the camp dates?


Our first camp session is scheduled for August 2014. It will be Friday afternoon through Monday morning.

What is the age requirement?

Anyone who is 21 and over is welcome to attend.

Where is the camp located?

Camp Unplugg’d will be taking place at the family-owned Friendly Pines Camp in Prescott, Arizona (and they live up to their name!). It is situated on 86 acres and high in the beautiful Bradshaw mountains. www.friendlypines.com

How much does it cost?

Depending on if you prefer a group cabin or private cabin, the range will be between $425-$525 per camper.

What does the fee cover?

Campers will receive:
Accommodations from Friday night to Monday morning
Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner (3 nights)
Activities, Workshops, and Amenities
Camp Unplugg’d T-shirt
Memories that will last a lifetime

What do I bring?

Sleeping bag/pillow/blanket
Toiletries
Change of clothes
Flashlight
An adventurous spirit

More Questions

Why would I need Camp Unplugg’d?

With our lives constantly moving and work never ending, we find this much more difficult to do in the real world. We challenge you to go a full weekend with no phone, tv, or wifi. It is nearly impossible. We are proving a safe and fun environment where you can unplug without distraction or impulse.

Why would I pay to turn off my phone?

1. Rejuvenation. Rest, and Relax. Clear your mind. Improve patience and attention span Communication.
2. New friends. Sharing experiences with like-minded people. Learning new skills.
3. Accountability. Power in numbers: unplugging will make it easier for you to stay unplugg’d too.

Can I bring a camera?

Yes, but only film, disposable, and Polaroid!

Will transportation be provided?

No. Your registration for Camp UnPlugg’d includes room, board, and activities. Getting up to camp is at the responsibility of the camper. However, we do encourage car pooling and will send out a list of ride shares and shuttle services from Phoenix.

I live in New York… Australia… on Pluto.. can I come?

Yes! All countries and alien-life forms are welcome.
Fun Fact: Pluto was discovered 2 hours away at Northern Arizona University (NAU) Lowell Observatory in Flagstaff, AZ. And yes, we still believe Pluto is a planet.

Can I share accommodation with my significant other?

Cabins are separated by gender, just like at camp. We like keep our numbers small, no more than 5-6 per cabin. There will also be a limited number of private cabins, if you wish to share with the opposite gender or prefer to bunk on your own (additional charge). We are also working on a space for you to BYOT, aka Bring Your Own Tent. Snorer? Let us know and we’ll bunk you with your kind.

What type of food will be served?

Healthy, camp choices will be served family and buffet style.
We will do our best to accommodate all dietary needs/restrictions in advance.

Can I bring an animal?

Service animals are welcome.

What airport is closest to you?

Sky-Harbor Airport in Phoenix, AZ., 2 hours from Prescott.

But wait! I need my phone that has my GPS to get me to camp. Can’t I just put it in my pocket for the weekend?

Nope. Everyone will “check in to check out.” We will check in all devices upon arrival so you can check out of technology. Devices will be secured safely and returned to you at the end of camp.

Sooo…In case of emergency... ?

We will have a camp phone number that we will give to our campers in case of emergency. This can be given to your friends and family. If you are still concerned, we recommend that you tell your work colleagues, clients, email contacts, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Linked In, and any other social media where will you be. Trust us, you’ll be okay.
